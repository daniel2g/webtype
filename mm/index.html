Hesitantly,

she

placed

her

hand

over

a

pale

orange

patch

in

the

upper

right

corner

of

the

map.

That

should

be

right.

Pasadena.

lifted

her

hand

and

looked

under

it,

then

folded

the

map

and

put

it

back

on

the

dashboard.

He

could

read,

she

realized

belatedly.

He

could

probably

write,

too.

Abruptly,

she

hated

him —

deep,

bitter

hatred.

What

did

literacy

mean

to

him —

a

grown

man

who

played

cops

and

robbers?

But

he

was

literate

and

she

was

not.
